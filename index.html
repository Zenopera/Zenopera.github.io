Projects

Master Thesis – Trivariate Non-Parametric Model

Developed a trivariate, data-driven model to analyze the effects of classroom environments on student performance. Each class was associated with a cluster representing positive or negative impact on student scores, estimated using an Expectation-Maximization algorithm. The methodology was inspired by research at Politecnico di Milano and applied in the context of Luxembourgish educational data.

The model has been successfully used at SCRIPT, Ministry of Education of Luxembourg.

Deep Learning – Blood Cell Classification

Collaborated on a multi-class image classification project to identify 8 types of blood cells from 96x96 RGB images. Started with a baseline CNN and progressed to transfer learning with pre-trained models, applying fine-tuning, data augmentation, and regularization to improve performance.

The final model (ConvNeXtSmall) reached 97.66% accuracy on local test data, providing practical experience in deep learning, model fine-tuning, and collaborative development.

My contribution: Selected layers for fine-tuning, refined augmentation strategies, and optimized the model pipeline.

Bayesian Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM)

Applied Bayesian Marketing Mix Modeling to analyze the impact of advertising, pricing, and competitor activities on sales in the fiber-optic telecommunications industry. The goal was to provide a probabilistic approach for optimizing marketing budget allocation.

Developed and compared three Bayesian models: one using Fourier basis functions to capture seasonality, one using Gaussian Processes for time-varying advertising effects, and one combining both approaches. Each model accounted for non-linear effects and diminishing returns.

This project provided hands-on experience in Bayesian statistics, time-series modeling, and probabilistic decision-making.
